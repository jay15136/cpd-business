# About This Site

## Purpose of the Site
The CPD Business Directory was created to give the Coraopolis Borough Police Department a single, dependable place to keep important business information in one location. Instead of relying on paper files, scattered notes, or staff memory, the department can use this site to quickly locate the information officers and dispatchers need during calls for service, follow-up investigations, and routine patrol work. [cite:2][cite:4]

The directory is designed for local police use, not for the public. Its purpose is practical: help staff find accurate business details faster, improve response readiness, and make sure important information is available when time matters. [cite:2][cite:4]

## Why the Directory Matters
Police departments often need business-related information for alarms, after-hours calls, suspicious activity complaints, open doors, key holder contact, camera follow-up, and hazardous material concerns. Keeping that information in a shared internal site helps reduce delays and gives officers and dispatchers a more consistent source of information. [cite:4]

For Coraopolis, this site also supports a more organized way to maintain business records over time. It creates one central location where updates can be entered as businesses change owners, hours, alarm companies, managers, or emergency contacts. [cite:2][cite:3][cite:4]

## What the Site Does
In plain terms, the website helps department staff:

- Search for a business by name, address, category, or type. [cite:5][cite:7]
- View important business details in one place, such as contact information, hours, managers, owners, key holders, alarm information, camera information, and hazard notes. [cite:3][cite:4]
- Add new business records when a new business opens or when a paper intake form is received. [cite:3][cite:4]
- Edit existing records when information changes, such as phone numbers, ownership, emergency contacts, or security details. [cite:3][cite:4]
- Organize businesses by category and business type so records are easier to sort, review, and find. [cite:5][cite:7]
- Provide quick-reference information that can help officers respond more effectively to calls involving businesses. [cite:4]
- Show summary information on a dashboard so staff can see what is in the system and identify records that may need review or updating. [cite:2]
- Track system activity so the department knows who signed in, what pages were viewed, what records were created or edited, and when users signed off. [cite:2]

## How It Helps Staff
This site is meant to be useful to both patrol and dispatch.

For patrol officers, it can reduce the time spent trying to locate a business owner, key holder, or manager during an incident. It can also help officers know whether a location has an alarm system, security cameras, special access issues, or possible hazards before they arrive or while they are on scene. [cite:4]

For dispatch personnel, it creates a faster way to confirm business information while handling calls. It can support better communication with officers in the field by giving dispatch a reliable internal reference for business contacts and site-specific details. [cite:2][cite:4]

## Benefits to the Police Department
Using this system can provide several department-wide benefits:

- Faster access to business information during active calls. [cite:4]
- Less dependence on paper files and individual staff memory. [cite:2][cite:4]
- More consistent recordkeeping across shifts and users. [cite:2][cite:3]
- Better follow-up on alarms, key holder notifications, and business-related incidents. [cite:4]
- Easier updating when business information changes. [cite:3][cite:4]
- Better awareness of security features such as alarm coverage and camera contacts. [cite:4]
- Better awareness of potential risks, including hazardous materials or special concerns noted for a location. [cite:4]
- Improved accountability through activity tracking and audit history. [cite:2]
- A stronger foundation for long-term records maintenance and future expansion. [cite:2][cite:5]

## Plain-Language Features
The site was built to be straightforward and practical for daily police use. In non-technical terms, its main features include:

- A main dashboard that gives staff a quick overview of the directory. [cite:2]
- A searchable business list so users can find locations quickly. [cite:5][cite:7]
- Business pages or records that hold contact, security, and response-related details in one place. [cite:3][cite:4]
- An admin area where authorized department users can add or update information. [cite:2]
- User sign-in tied to Windows network accounts so activity is connected to the employee using the site. [cite:2]
- Access to the audit log limited to supervisors, while regular users can still use and update the directory. [cite:2]
- Tracking of logins, page views, edits, new records, and logoffs so the department has a record of site use. [cite:2]
- A local-network setup so the site stays inside the department environment rather than being exposed to the public internet. [cite:2]

## Why This Approach Fits CPD
For a department like Coraopolis, a local internal business directory is a practical tool because it supports everyday operations without requiring a large, complex outside system. It focuses on information the department actually uses: who to call, who has access, what security is present, and what officers should know before or during a response. [cite:2][cite:4]

This also matches the department's need for a controlled internal reference system that staff can maintain themselves over time. The value of the site is not just in storing information, but in making that information easier to find, easier to update, and easier to use during real police work. [cite:2][cite:3][cite:4]

## Possible Next Steps
As the site continues to develop, the next steps could include:

- Reviewing all business categories and business types to keep them organized and consistent. [cite:5][cite:7]
- Continuing to collect intake forms from local businesses so the directory becomes more complete over time. [cite:3][cite:4]
- Setting a regular schedule for record review so outdated contacts and stale records can be corrected. [cite:3][cite:4]
- Training staff on how and when to update records so entries stay accurate. [cite:2]
- Creating a simple maintenance routine for backups, updates, and supervisor review of audit activity. [cite:2]
- Expanding reporting or dashboard views so staff can identify missing information or businesses that have not been reviewed recently. [cite:2]
- Developing written internal procedures that explain who is responsible for entering, reviewing, and approving business information. [cite:2][cite:3]

## Possible Future Issues
Like any internal records system, this site could face challenges if it is not maintained regularly.

Possible issues include:

- Outdated business records if staff do not update information after ownership, phone numbers, managers, alarm providers, or hours change. [cite:3][cite:4]
- Incomplete records if intake forms are missing fields or are never entered into the system. [cite:3][cite:4]
- Inconsistent entry practices if users are not trained to enter information the same way. [cite:2][cite:3]
- Access-control concerns if user permissions are not reviewed or supervisor-only functions are not monitored. [cite:2]
- Audit log growth and record-management issues over time if review and retention practices are not clearly defined. [cite:2]
- Attachment or file-size issues if staff begin using larger supporting documents than the system was originally set up to handle. [cite:2]
- Dependence on staff follow-through, since the system is only as accurate as the information entered and maintained. [cite:3][cite:4]

The CPD Business Directory is intended to be a practical working tool for the Coraopolis Borough Police Department. Its value comes from helping officers and dispatchers get useful business information quickly, keep records in one place, and maintain better awareness of contacts, security details, and special concerns tied to local businesses. [cite:2][cite:4]

With regular updates, clear procedures, and continued staff use, the site can become a strong long-term operational resource for the department. [cite:2][cite:3]
